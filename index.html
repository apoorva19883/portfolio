About me

I came for the code.
I stayed for the design.

I started out studying software development at SAIT — and somewhere between writing code and using badly designed apps every single day, I realised what I actually wanted to do was make things feel right. Not just work. Feel right.

That switch wasn't accidental. I grew up drawing, painting, crocheting — I've always made things with my hands. When I discovered UX/UI design it felt like the first time my technical brain and my creative brain were finally working in the same direction.

Every project I've built comes from something I genuinely felt. Bleed came from the quiet guilt of paying for things I never use. Noxora came from being a K-drama fan who was tired of Netflix burying the good stuff. MixRight came from painting and not knowing how to recreate a colour I loved. Verdant came from killing plants and wishing something could tell me why — and Looply is coming soon, a crochet learning game that combines my love of crafting with gamification design.

I think the best design comes from actually caring about the problem. Not just studying it — living it.

When I'm not designing: you'll find me mid-crochet project, mixing paint colours I probably can't recreate, or three episodes deep into a drama I swore I'd only watch one episode of.
